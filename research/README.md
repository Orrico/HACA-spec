# HACA Research & Theory Archive

This directory contains deep-dive documentation, theoretical explorations, and early architecture drafts that informed the development of HACA v1.0.

## Purpose

While the documents in this directory are **non-normative** and have been superseded by the official specifications in the `/spec` directory, they represent a significant body of research. They are preserved here to:

- Provide deep theoretical context for architectural decisions.
- Document edge cases and complex scenarios explored during the design phase.
- Serve as a reference for future extensions (e.g., HACA-Security, HACA-Symbiont/Evolve deep dives).
- Preserve the "deep-prose" explanations that were distilled into the clean RFC-style specifications.

## Contents

- **[spec/](spec/)**: Detailed (and often very dense) versions of the core components.
- **[drafts/](drafts/)**: Early Internet-Draft style compositions.

## How to use this archive

If you find a section in the official HACA v1.0 specification that seems overly simplified, you will likely find the detailed rationale and expanded research here. This is the "brain dump" from which the standard was forged.

---

*Note: For implementation or compliance purposes, always refer to the [official specifications](../spec).*
